Chem-E Car

Envision's flagship project, Chem-E car is an international competition hosted by the American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) and bands together engineers from multiple disciplines to solve a simple but difficult engineering problem: Can a shoebox-sized car, only powered and stopped by chemical reactions, be designed to transport any weight over any distance?

Beer brewing project

Wouldn't you like to tailor an IPA to the hopiness to your choosing, affordably and consistently? And wouldn't you want to do it in the comfort of your own home? The beer brewing project combines process control and the Internet-Of-Things (IOT) in a user-friendly package, potentially allowing any user to be brewmasters, anywhere.

Flow Cell

A flow battery is a type of rechargeable battery where rechargeability is provided by two chemicals contained within the system and separated by a membrane where ion exchange occurs. Interest has been expressed in deploying a microgrid on-campus as a pilot project, with potential scale-up being managed by UBC Envision.

Envision Flow Cell

Algae Project

The algae project is a foray into understanding the world's energy needs and the requirements needed to sustain it. This has led to an initiative to learn about microalgae biochemicals and biofuels, focusing on rethinking photobioreactor design to optimize cell growth and fuel yield.
