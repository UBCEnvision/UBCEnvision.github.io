What to we do?

We are inherently problem-solvers, and in news there are many problems that have moved from being an abstract, happening in some far away country, to problems we experience and face that have shaped how we live, and will shape how we will live. By being an engineering design team, we had the support to branch out from our flagship ChemE Car project to an array of 4 total projects that foster the engineering mindset, even at the design team level.

UBC ChemE Car
Envision started with ChemE Car and we are not about to forget our roots. ChemE Car is an annual competition that is held by the American Institute of Chemical Engineers, and tasks us with delivering a payload of random weight a random distance on our car, both of which are not told to us until right before the competition. Caveat: The car can only be powered and stopped using chemical reactions.

Automated Beer Brewing
Envision’s beer brewing is built from the ground up because we aim to “teach” a microcontroller brew recipes through machine learning. We collect data from our brews and feed them back into our circuits, flagging correct and wrong parameters. This will help breweries, commercial and home brewers alike, increase the efficiency of their processes since the machine has been bred all throughout the various iterations of the brew to recognize and control the right parameters, instead of needing extra coding done at the end of the process.

Flow Cell Battery
The flow cell project aims to investigate the viability of using flow-assisted zinc-nickel hydroxide batteries as a form of energy storage. The flowing electrolyte in a flow cell makes it more energy efficient and last longer. Moreover, normal batteries have more harmful metals (e.g. lead-acid batteries). Our cell uses potassium hydroxide and zinc oxide solutions which are non-toxic. With the flow cell, we want to vary combinations of the cell's parameters - flow regime, cell geometry, electrode shape, and others, in order to optimize the cell's performance and, eventually, build a microgrid of flow cells on campus.

Algae Biofuels
The algae project aims to test the feasibility, optimum growing and extracting condition for combining algal wastewater treatment with nutrient removal for biofuel production. Currently, two types of biofuels can be extracted from algae; cytosolic lipid bodies contain large amounts of triacylglycerides (TAG), which can be processed into biodiesel via transesterification, and monomeric sugars can be fermented into bioethanol. The project will involve attempts to extract lipids and/or sugars from microalgae used in wastewater treatment.

How can I be part of Envision?

1. First thing’s first, you need to sign up for our mailing list to receive updates, and apply for an interview with our online form (Links below).

2. We split our interviews into two parts: an individual and a group portion.

a. In the individual portion, behavioural questions (e.g. what challenges have you faced in your academic career?) will be asked, allowing us to get to know your personality.

b. In the group portion, you will be randomly put into groups of 6-8, and we will ask you a question, which would have been sent to you 3 days beforehand. We ask that the group discuss the question amongst themselves until they agree on a single answer. Cooperation, mutual understanding and critical thinking are tested in this part of the interview.

We will contact you with an interview package through email. In the interview package will be your time slot and date, location and a question. This question will be asked during the group interview session. Sending it beforehand allows everyone to ponder and come up with compelling and valid reasons to support their points.

3. Wait for our response! We hope to get back to everyone 1 week after their respective interview times.
